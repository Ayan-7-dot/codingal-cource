Macronutrients

Macronutrients are the nutrients the body needs in large amounts to produce energy. They include carbohydrates, proteins, and fats.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's main source of energy. They are found in foods such as rice, bread, fruits, and vegetables.

Proteins

Proteins help build and repair tissues in the body. Good sources include eggs, meat, fish, legumes, and dairy products.

Fats

Fats provide energy and help the body absorb certain vitamins. Healthy fats can be found in nuts, seeds, avocados, and olive oil.

Side note: Not all fats are equal — trans fats and excess saturated fats are linked to health risks, while unsaturated fats support heart health.

Micronutrients

Micronutrients are vitamins and minerals the body needs in smaller amounts, but they are still essential for proper growth, immunity, and overall function.

Side note: Eating a variety of colors in fruits and vegetables usually means a wider range of vitamins and antioxidants.

Balanced Diet

A balanced diet combines the right proportions of macronutrients and micronutrients to support energy levels, growth, and long-term health. Learn more from the World Health Organization's guide to a healthy diet.

Hydration

Water is essential for digestion, circulation, and regulating body temperature. Most health guidelines recommend drinking water consistently throughout the day rather than waiting until feeling thirsty.
